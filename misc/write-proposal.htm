The following elements are used when a proposal is reviewed by the NSF, therefore, a successful proposal must explicitly address all of them.
  1. What is the potential for the proposed activity to:
    1. Advance knowledge and understanding within its own field or across different fields (Intellectual Merit); and
    2. Benefit society or advance desired societal outcomes (Broader Impacts)?
  2. To what extent do the proposed activities suggest and explore creative, original, or potentially transformative concepts?
  3. Is the plan for carrying out the proposed activities well-reasoned, well-organized, and based on a sound rationale?
  4. Does the plan incorporate a mechanism to assess success?
  5. How well qualified is the individual, team, or organization to conduct the proposed activities?
  6. Are there adequate resources available to the PI (either at the home organization or through collaborations) to carry out the proposed activities?
Intellectual Merits
  1. Important/significance of the project
  2. Qualitification of PIs
  3. Creativity/originality/innovation/transformative potential of the methods and approaches -- e.g. move the field into a new direction (transformative), or advance the state-of-the-art in a novel and innovative way. Some example descriptions: "There are potentially transformative apsects of the proposed work, which if successful could ...", or, "These activities are potentailly transformative because ..."
  4. Well-conceived and organized methods and approaches
  5. Resource adaquacy
Broader Impacts
  1. Advance discovery and understanding while promoting teaching and learning
    • Develop and contribute educational materials based on your research that you can include in a teaching database like a K-16 digital library;
    • Involve K-12, undergraduate or graduate students as participants in your research;
    • Integrate your project activities into K-12, undergraduate and graduate teaching of science, math and engineering;
    • Collaborate with educators and researchers to incorporate your research into learning and education;
    • Involve your graduate and post-doctoral student researchers in undergraduate teaching activities;
    • Invite student teams involved in your research to make presentations to professional societies;
    • Establish mentoring programs for high school, undergraduate and graduate students, as well as research technicians; or
    • Involve your team and your project activities in the recruitment, training or professional development of K-12 math and science teachers.
  2. Promote participation of under-represented groups
  3. Enhance infrastructure for research and education
  4. Broad dissemination
  5. Societal benefits beyond the directly related technical field
In the proposal, try to use statements or sentences that reviewers can use to write his/her reviews. An example proposal summary is shown below.

The intellectual merit of the project is its transformational approach, which integrates modeling the fate of a micropollutant in a biological process with a fundamental co-substrate mechanism, bioselector design effects, and microbial composition based on molecular methods. A second potential far reaching benefit will be the development of pure cultures capable of EE2 degradation under conditions similar to a wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) for future kinetic and genetic studies, and development of qPCR primer sets for monitoring select EE2-degrading heterotrophs in WWTP facilities.

The broader impacts of the project are benefits to society by providing a basis to optimize WWTP biotreatment design to minimize estrogen release to the environment. Direct educational benefits include the training of graduate students, and participation of undergraduate researchers. Increased participation by under-represented groups will be realized through established university programs and continued partnership with the Office of Diversity.
