NSF Proposal Summary -- A General Structure

NSF requires a proposal summary to explicitly include two sections: Intellectual Merits and Broader Impacts. The summary structure below follows that requirements.

Intellectual Merits

  1. Develop the broadest context for the proposed research
  2. State research questions: hypothese to test, objectives to achieve, etc.
  3. Identify gaps in current knowledge and state-of-the-art, and state how the proposed research will fill those gaps
  4. Include preliminary work of your own to make a case for the proposed research
  5. Outline the proposed work or activities to perform

Broader Impacts

  1. Outline projected outputs and results from the proposal
  2. State how the proposed research will advance the sibject area, and its implications for other fields or problems
  3. State broad societal impacts
  4. State how the proposal will advance discovery and understanding while proomoting learning and education
